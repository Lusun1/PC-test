Mounting the Recoater Blade

Before starting the build, there are several preparation steps: mounting the recoater blade, mounting and leveling the building platform, cleaning the lens and double checking all set up conditions. In this chapter, we will walk through the steps to mount the recoater blade.

The recoater blade is important in the building process because it allows the recoater to spread a layer of powder evenly. There are three different types of recoater blades. Selection of the recoater blade type depends upon the details of the build including the design and the powder material.

  1. HSS or High Speed Steel blade
  2. Brush recoater blade
  3. Ceramic recoater blade

The recoater blade is attached under the recoater using a blade holder. There are three steps to mounting recoater blade:
Step1. Select the correct recoater blade and ride the recoater blade on the recoater housing.
Step2. Place the blade holder on the recoater housing.
Step3. Attach the blade to the blade holder using the screws.

The HSS or High Speed Steel recoater blade is used most often. It is a tough blade that will hold up a long time.


The brush recoater blade is used on thin wall or delicate builds. The soft brush is less likely to damage the build than a hard recoater blade.


The ceramic recoater blade is used if the powder has a magnetic charge. The magnetic powder will not adhere to the ceramic.
